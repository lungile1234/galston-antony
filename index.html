The Journey

Galston Anthony is not a career politician. He is a creative entrepreneur and systems builder with a diverse background spanning business, infrastructure, media, and property development.

As a systems thinker, Galston approaches complex urban challenges with the mindset of an engineer and the heart of a builder. He understands that Johannesburg's problems are interconnected and require holistic, sustainable solutions.

"Government should be run like a successful business: with clear budgets, measurable KPIs, strict timelines, and an unwavering focus on delivery. Johannesburg doesn't need more politicians; it needs competent builders who understand systems."

— Galston Anthony

Now, as mayoral candidate for Johannesburg 2026, Galston brings his unique perspective as a builder and systems thinker to the task of restoring competence, accountability, and dignity to South Africa's economic heartland.

The Philosophy

Galston's approach is built on three core principles:

1. Systems Over Silos

Johannesburg's challenges are interconnected. Crime affects investment, which affects employment, which affects housing. Galston believes in addressing these as interconnected systems.

2. Competence Over Politics

In a city bleeding from decades of mismanagement, political gamesmanship must take a backseat to competent governance.

3. Building Over Talking

While others make promises, Galston focuses on building. Real change comes from implementation, not just intention.

The Vision

Galston's vision for Johannesburg is simple yet transformative: to restore the city to its rightful place as the economic engine of Africa—a city that works for all its residents.
